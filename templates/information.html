Information

Why Backup Your Media Content?

Content Preservation

YouTube videos can be removed for various reasons: copyright claims, channel termination, or the creator themselves may delete content. Having local backups ensures you always have access to important content.

Platform Independence

Relying solely on third-party platforms means your access depends on their policies and continued existence. Local backups give you control over your media library.

Offline Access

Backed up content can be accessed without an internet connection, which is useful during travel, in areas with poor connectivity, or when you want to save on data usage.

Quality Preservation

YouTube may change video quality or compression methods over time. Creating backups allows you to preserve content at your preferred quality level.

For Content Creators

If you're a content creator, keeping backups of your own videos is essential. Platform policies can change, and having your original content locally stored provides peace of mind and allows for re-uploading if necessary.

How to Use This Tool

Basic Usage

  1. Copy the YouTube URL of the video or playlist you want to back up.
  2. Paste the URL into the input field on the home page.
  3. Select either "Audio" or "Video" format.
  4. Choose a destination folder for the downloaded files.
  5. Click "Begin Backup" to start the download process.

Playlist vs. Single Video

When you paste a URL that's part of a playlist, you'll be asked whether you want to download just that video or the entire playlist. If you choose to download a playlist, a new folder will be created to organize all the files.

Understanding Errors and Warnings

Sometimes, you might encounter errors or warnings during the download process. Here's what they mean:

Message Meaning Solution
"Some files were not backed up" The download completed, but not all files in a playlist were successfully downloaded. The successfully downloaded files are still available in the destination folder. You can retry the specific failed items.
"Error: Video unavailable" The video might be private, removed, or region-restricted. Try using a VPN or check if the video is still available on YouTube.
"Trying alternative download method..." The primary download method failed, and the application is attempting a backup method. This is normal and doesn't require action.

File Formats

This tool downloads audio files as MP3 and video files as MP4. The file names are based on the original video titles with special characters removed and spaces replaced with dashes.

Important Note

Always respect copyright laws and terms of service. This tool is intended for personal backup purposes only. Please use it responsibly and ethically.
